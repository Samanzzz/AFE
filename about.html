About Dr. Abolfazl Eslami

Dr. Abolfazl Eslami received Ph.D. degree from the University of Ottawa in 1996, specializing in deep foundations. His research fellowship was associated at the University of British Columbia (UBC) in 1994 on the Cone Penetration Test (CPT) and Piezocone (CPTu) state of the practice. He completed B.Sc. in Civil Engineering at Sharif University of Technology (SUT) in 1985 and received M.Sc. in Geotechnical Engineering at Tehran Polytechnic (AUT) in 1988. He has been a professor at the Universities of Guilan and Amirkabir University of Technology from 1997 to 2024. He taught various postgraduate courses, such as Advanced Foundation Engineering, Ground Improvement, and Marine Geotechnical Engineering. Over the course of his research career, Dr. Eslami has supervised 150 M.Sc. and 25 Ph.D. theses. He has gained and extended practical experience regionally and overseas as founder and chairman of Sham-e Consulting Engineering Co. The assignments covered various residential buildings, industrial plants, bridges, highways, and marine structures. He has advanced and shared his expertise as a visiting scholar and fellow at several Universities, including McGill, UNLV, UCSD, and CUMT (2017–2024). Dr. Eslami is an internationally recognized multidisciplinary authority member of prestigious institutes, including IGS, DFI, ISSMGE, and ASCE, serving frequently as an invited speaker, organizer, and peer reviewer.

The significant accomplishments of Dr. Eslami are as follows:

- Development of the Unicone Program (Eslami and Fellenius Method, 1997), implemented worldwide for pile design and soil behavior classification using CPT and CPTu records.

- Compilation of AUT; Geo-CPT & Pile Database; 600 records of pile axial loading tests and adjacent CPT (CPTu) profiles leading to Relevant Data-Based Design (RDBD).

- Establishment of a physical modeling lab securing three national invention patents; emphasis on Frustum Confining Vessel (FCV-AUT) fabrication: large-scale and high-stress device for penetrometers, helical and special piles performance assessment.

- Published over 150 peer-reviewed journal papers in well-known international journals, including DFI, CGJ, SAOS, SDEE, ICE, MGG, Georisk, ASCE, GGE, BEE, PEM, TRR, TRIG and OE.

- Delivered several keynote lectures, workshops, and presentations on topics of CPT & CPTu applications, special foundation systems, adjacent buildings construction complications, physical modeling, ground improvement, and sustainable geostructures.

- Authored two texts and source books published by worldwide recognized publishers, Elsevier and Wiley, titled "Piezocone and Cone Penetration Test (CPTu and CPT) Applications in Foundation Engineering (2020)" and "Advanced Foundation Engineering, Principles, Performance and Prospect (2025)." Also, contributed to two chapters of the "Databases for Data-Centric Geotechnics (2024)" published by CRC Press.

- Novel categorization of foundation systems upon form and load transfer aspects emphasizing foundation performance under combined (VMH) loading towards smart selection of geosystems.

- Promoting the FELADD package, comprising load-displacement records for different foundation types, including small-scale, footings, piles, pile groups, and PRFs, toward energy-based capacity appraisal for efficient and optimum design.

- Principal investigator, chief designer, and peer reviewer of dozens geostructures and ground engineering projects focusing on value engineering and sustainable development.

- Honored and awarded as the top national engineer by the ISSCE and for lifetime achievement in Civil Engineering from the National Foundation of Lasting Fame (2015) and superior professor (2017).
